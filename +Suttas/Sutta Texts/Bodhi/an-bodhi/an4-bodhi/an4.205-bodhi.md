# AN4.205 Eightfold

> **Division:** I. THE GOOD PERSON

\[Opening as in 4:201.\]

(1) “And who, bhikkhus, is the bad person? Here, someone is of wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, and wrong samādhi. \[221\] This is called the bad person.

(2) “And who is the person inferior to the bad person? Here, someone is himself of wrong view and encourages others in wrong view … he is himself of wrong samādhi and encourages others in wrong samādhi. This is called the person inferior to the bad person.

(3) “And who is the good person? Here, someone is of right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right samādhi. This is called the good person.

(4) “And who is the person superior to the good person? Here, someone is himself of right view and encourages others in right view … he is himself of right samādhi and encourages others in right samādhi. This is called the person superior to the good person.”
