# 4:10  Before the Break-up (of the Body)

“Seeing how,

behaving how,

is one said to be

at peace?

Gotama, tell me about

—when asked about—

the ultimate person.”

The Buddha:

“Free from craving

before the break-up

[of the body],

independent

of _before_

& _the end,_ [1](#^snp4-10-01) ^snp4-10-01-ref

not classified _in between,_ [2](#^snp4-10-02) ^snp4-10-02-ref

no preference is his.

Un-          angered,

un-          startled,

un-          boastful,

un-          anxious,

giving counsel unruffled,

_he_ is a sage,

his speech

under control.

Free from attachment

with regard to the future,

not sorrowing

over the past,

he sees seclusion

in the midst of sensory contacts.[3](#^snp4-10-03) ^snp4-10-03-ref

He can’t be led

in terms of views.[4](#^snp4-10-04) ^snp4-10-04-ref

Withdrawn, un-

deceitful, not

stingy, not

miserly, not

insolent, in-

offensive,

he doesn’t engage in

divisive speech.

Not drunk on enticements,

nor given to pride,

he’s gentle, quick-witted,

beyond conviction & dispassion.[5](#^snp4-10-05) ^snp4-10-05-ref

Not in hopes of material gain

does he take on the training;

when without material gain

he isn’t upset.

Unobstructed     by craving,

he doesn’t               through craving[6](#^snp4-10-06) ^snp4-10-06-ref

hunger for flavors.

Equanimous—always—mindful,

he doesn’t suppose himself

equal,

superior,

inferior,

in the world.

No swellings of pride

are his.

Whose dependencies

don’t exist

when, on knowing the Dhamma,

he’s in-

dependent;

in whom no craving is found

for becoming or not-:

_He_ is said

to be at peace,

un-intent

on sensual pleasures,

with nothing at all

to tie him down:

one who’s crossed over attachment.

He has no

children

cattle,

fields,

land.

In him you can’t pin down

what’s embraced

or rejected.[7](#^snp4-10-07) ^snp4-10-07-ref

He has no preference

for that which people run-of-the-mill

or brahmans & contemplatives

might blame—

which is why

he is unperturbed

with regard to their words.

His greed gone,

not miserly,

the sage

doesn’t speak of himself

as among those who are higher,

equal,

or lower.

He,

theory-free,

goes to no theory.

For whom

nothing in the world

is his own,

who doesn’t grieve

over what is not,

who doesn’t enter into

doctrines

phenomena[8](#^snp4-10-08): ^snp4-10-08-ref

_He_ is said

to be

at peace.”

vv. 848–861

### Notes

1. Nd I: “Independent of before & the end” = no craving or view with regard to past or future. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-01-ref) ^snp4-10-01

2. For discussions of how the awakened one cannot be classified even in the present, see [[mn72-thanissaro]] and [[sn22-34-thanissaro/sn22-thanissaro/sn22.85-thanissaro]]. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-02-ref) ^snp4-10-02

3. Nd I: “He sees seclusion in the midst of sensory contacts” = he sees contact as empty of self. This passage may also refer to the fact that the awakened person experiences sensory contact as if disjoined from it. On this point, see [[mn140-thanissaro]] and [[mn146-thanissaro]], quoted in _The Mind Like Fire Unbound,_ chapter 4. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-03-ref) ^snp4-10-03

4. See [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro no notes/an-thanissaro/an10/an10.93-thanissaro]]. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-04-ref) ^snp4-10-04

5. Beyond conviction & dispassion—The Pali here can also mean, “A person of no conviction, he does not put away passion.” This is an example of the kind of pun occasionally used in Pali poetry for its shock value. Other examples are at [Dhp 97](../Dhp/Ch07.html#dhp097) and the end of [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.13-thanissaro]]. For examples of what is meant by being beyond conviction, see [[sn12-21-thanissaro/sn12-thanissaro/sn12.68-thanissaro]] and [[sn48.44-thanissaro]]. For an explanation of what is meant by being beyond dispassion, see [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.6-thanissaro]]. An alternate explanation is that, as [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro no notes/snp-thanissaro/snp5/snp5.6-thanissaro]] indicates, the arahant is beyond all dhammas, dispassion included. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-05-ref) ^snp4-10-05

6. The Pali word _taṇhāya_—by/through craving—functions here as a lamp. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-06-ref) ^snp4-10-06

7. This reading follows the Thai and PTS editions: _attaṁ vā-pi nirattaṁ vā._ The Burmese and Sri Lankan editions read, _attā vā-pi nirattā vā:_ “self or what’s opposed to self.” The first reading seems preferable for two reasons: First, it follows the theme established in [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.3-thanissaro]] and [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.4-thanissaro]] (and also followed in [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.15-thanissaro]] and [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro no notes/snp-thanissaro/snp5/snp5.11-thanissaro]]) that the awakened person has gone beyond embracing or rejecting views. Second, the word _nirattā_ is found nowhere else in the Canon aside from the two other verses in Sn ([4:3](StNp4_3.html) and [4:14](StNp4_14.html)) where it is offered as a possible alternative reading for _niratta_ (released, rejected). As _niratta_ is clearly the preferable alternative in [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.3-thanissaro]], I have adopted it here and in [[+Suttas/Sutta Texts/Thanissaro/Thanissaro notes/snp-thanissaro/snp4-thanissaro/snp4.14-thanissaro]] as well. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-07-ref) ^snp4-10-07

8. “Doctrines, phenomena”—two meanings of the Pali word, _dhamma_. [↩︎](#^snp4-10-08-ref) ^snp4-10-08

> **Edition Notice**  
Bhikkhu Thanissaro’s translations are arranged according to the Thai Tipitaka edition. Therefore, some sutta references may not align with the 6th Council or Suttacentral.net numbering schemes, especially in the Saṁyutta Nikāya (SN) and Aṅguttara Nikāya (AN).
